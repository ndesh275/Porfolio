About Me

I'm Brian and i'm a Web Designer

I'm sitting at home, in the heart of Kiambu, having a cup of coffee, tapping away at my laptop while it rains outside. In a few years I will walk into my first job as a front-end developer. 2 months ago I was just a student at Baricho High School who knew nothing about programming, and now I'm here. How did this happen?” Come along with me as I share with you my journey of how I got here.

Feeling Special

When I first started thinking about becoming a developer, I would read articles like this one with a bit of skepticism. I kept on looking for something in the writer's background that made them “special”. That made them suited for this course. Something that I knew I didn't have. I have since come to understand that this is not how it works. There aren't any “special” requirements to becoming a developer. I'm not going to tell you it's easy, because it isn't. But the good news is that all the requirements are things that are in everyone's reach. You have to be willing to work hard, learn a lot, and be consistent. You need to persist when things get tough. Talk yourself out of the moments of desperation when you feel like you are not cut out for this. That's all it takes, and everyone can do these things with a bit of practice. I started with no related background study. I had no money to spend on expensive courses, But thanks to God, I was among the Students sponsored from our the High School To go and study In Zaleogo academy. In my Mind, I knew That Everyone's circumstances were different, but I learned that if you put your mind to it, you can do it.

One True Path To Success

This clearly doesn't exist. Two of us started around the same time learning to code for a career change: myself and a good friend of mine. The two of us are now working in the field, and there is very little in common between our approaches. Each one followed the material and methods that worked for them. If there is one thing in common between us, it is that we simply did not give up, and kept on going. We all put a lot of hard work and persistence. So instead of the one true path, I'm going to tell you a bit about my path. It's not the only way, it is not the best way. It is what worked for me.

Getting Started

First, before i started attending my classes at Zalego Academy, I started reading about Web Development then i started watching YouTube videos about programming to understand where to begin. Then I started playing around with HTML and CSS. I followed tutorials and coded along to build some basic web pages. This made me see that it was something I really wanted to get into.

Learning

I knew that the only way forward was to study for it. I had full-time studying timetable for school, so waiting until “I had time” was never an option. I had to do it while still revising my school work and doing assignments, or not at all. I was lucky that my friend was learning at the same time. This helped us organize our days to maximize learning time. Then as April approached, seeing my deadline looming closer, I rallied again and got organized. I started pushing through, putting in those hours no matter how tired I was and how little time I had. Sometimes I would get up early to code, sometimes I would stay up late. This meant that my life was pretty much reduced to doing the work at school that i was dedicated also to do And I basically maintained that rhythm all the way until the day I started coding without refering anywhere. That was in the end of March, several weeks before my deadline.

Courses

What I believe is that Courses can only take you so far, so I complemented this with other tools to help me learn and in the future get a job.
GitHub: an important tool to learn, and where I host all my projects for free. And I soon came to realise that It's usually the first place employers look to see your work.
Portfolio: I learned the most vital development skills by building it and other self-initiated projects.

Getting a new Job

And so, on that rainy day at home, after finishing my coffee. I slept and dreamt that I walked into an office in the business area, introduced myself as the new front-end developer, and started my new career.Which i believe will soon become True.

End Of Story

I hope you enjoyed my story. If you did, you would make me happy if you give it some of those tasty claps.
